Assassin's Creed Valhalla announced

May 1st, 2020


The first trailer to announce Ubisoft's upcoming Assassin's Creed Valhalla has been released showcasing a new entry to the franchise set during the age of vikings. The CGI trailer begins with the the voice of a man narrating the vikings as violent barbarians while showing said vikings along with the main chracter doing the exact opposite. The narrater is Anglo-Saxon, people who have a real history of conflict with the vikings centuries ago. He states that they need to speak with them "in a language that they understand". The trailer next shows a battle between vikings and anglo-saxons. The main character, named Eivor, sees an older viking get cut down. He rushes to save him only for it to be too late. Eivor then faces the Anglo-Saxon's best warrior. When the battle turns against him, he uses the iconic hidden blade to win. The game promises a choice between a male or female Eivor with customization options both cosmetic and gameplay changing in scope. The player can make alliances, decide how to combat enemies, and change the world with dialogue choices. Assasin's Creed Valhalla will release on PS4, PS5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X, PC and Stadia.

Bionicle: Quest for Mata Nui recently announced

May 1st, 2020

In recent weeks a new fan-made project has been revealed that plans to revive a long lost line of LEGO toys. During the early 2000's LEGO intruduced the world to Bionicle, a line of buildable action figures with a history that stretched over a decade long before cancellation. For many who remeber Bionicle the main thing that set it apart from other LEGO products was its marketing campaign that featured a simple yet captivating narrative to go along with each toy. This was conveyed through CDs, flash games, and comic books. Bionicle was incredibly popular during it's time, garnering a fanbase that still remains nostalgic for it to this day. Bionicle: Quest for Mata Nui is advertised as an open-world rpg with plenty of exploration, quests and non-playable characters to offer. It also promises a dynamic combat system with an emphasis on combos and the unique powers of collectable masks found throughout the environment. The project has been in development for six years with a developer named "Crainy" leading the charge. His interview highlights his history working on the game among other topics. Overall, I'm very excited for this project. I recall enjoying Bionicle during my childhood and I hope this project can bring this lost era back to life.
